A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning

I believe that curiosity is one of my strengths when it comes to learning. I become curious about a subject when I'm interested in it, and I can easily spend a lot of time and effort learning more about it and trying to understand it. My openness and willingness to try new things are other of my strengths. It is very simple to become intimidated by learning something new, but because I believe learning is an ongoing process, I have found it to be exciting. We engage in a never-ending cycle of learning new things from birth until death. It is essential to our way of life. Our sense of perspective is expanded and altered as a result of experiencing new things in our lives. When it comes to learning limitations, procrastination is my biggest one. Procrastination has often served as a coping mechanism for me when I am overwhelmed or anxious about my academic performance. Although I understand logically that procrastinating does not help but only exacerbates my problems, I've discovered that it has often served as a default emotional coping mechanism for me. Another weakness of mine is that I often find it difficult to ask for help because I have a strong sense of independence and much prefer to deal with things on my own. This has had a negative impact on my learning because it causes me to struggle unnecessarily on my own even though there are resources and help available, which leads to me feeling stuck and causes me to take longer than necessary to complete certain tasks.

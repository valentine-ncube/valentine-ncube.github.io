What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

Values, empathy, and self-awareness all have a significant impact on the learning and programming process. Values serve as an ethical framework and goal orientation, ensuring that software adheres to ethical standards such as privacy, fairness, and transparency. Empathy allows developers to understand their users' needs, promoting user-friendly software and effective teamwork. Self-awareness assists students in understanding their own strengths and weaknesses, thereby promoting effective learning strategies. It also helps in recognising biases that can affect code outcomes in programming. Despite not being part of the code, these characteristics influence our programming approach, team interactions, and societal impact.

The most difficult challenge for me has been confronting myself, particularly regarding my weaknesses and limitations, as well as attempting to implement new learning strategies while unlearning old habits and strategies that no longer serve me.


Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

I believe this is because core learning aids in the development of soft skills required in programming. A good programmer possesses both technical and soft skills. Soft skills are just as vital to programmers as technical skills because they improve collaboration, communication, and problem-solving. While technical skills are obviously crucial to programming, human skills are what allow programmers to use their technical skills effectively, collaborate effectively with others, and create software that benefits users and society.
